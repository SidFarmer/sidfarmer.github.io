Hello, World!

01/01/2023


Today seems like as good as a day as any to formally outline my plans for the upcoming year.

In 2019 I created a YouTube channel and began uploading videos of myself learning to make AR filters for Instagram & Facebook. After two years I'd attracted a small but supportive audience and even started to monetise my efforts through YouTube's partner program and digital product sales on Gumroad. I was reaching out to potential clients and building a reputation within this rapidly growing community. But as more people entered the space, showcasing effects on every platform with AR capabilities that far exceeded anything I'd made up to that point I lost faith in my own skills and my output slowed. I felt I had no right making tutorials showing off the things I was learning, playing the part of teacher for thousands of people online when there were so many others more qualified than myself who could do a much better job. So I gave up.

That was over a year ago now and to this day it's one of my biggest regrets. Nothing else in near memory has engaged my focus for such an uninterrupted period of time or provided as much personal satisfaction as learning something new each day, coming up with a creative idea and implementing it in a way that could be shared with other like-minded individuals. In all honesty I miss the entire experience. And then by some morbid serendipity, just as I'd reached a point of personal reflection and come to realise what it was I'd let pass me by, someone claiming to work for one of those large tech companies we all express our opinions about using their own products slid into my DMs and made me an offer that quite frankly I would have been completely insane to refuse. An opportunity to teach those same things I'd started learning back in 2019, in an educational role making videos that would be uploaded directly to their platform. It seemed too good to be true.

And that's why in December of 2022 I paid far too much for a year long membership to Codecademy and have made a personal commitment that by the end of this year I'll be skilled enough to get a job doing something cool in tech. So far I've completed 11 courses on their site and plan on learning as much as possible during the rest of the year. It's been almost a decade since I first taught myself Javascript and as it's the language required for creating AR effects on Snapchat, TikTok, Facebook & Instagram that's been the main focus so far but I've also refreshed my knowledge of web design and database management. I've had a lot of fun re-learning HTML & CSS for what seems like the millionth time and this may be the most progress I've made so far in my ten year dalliance with computer programming.

I've always considered myself an amateur, learning new things for fun and hoping that one day the skills I pick up will come in handy for something, but over the past few years I've not thought about much else besides making cool stuff in cutting edge digital environments and sharing my progress on YouTube with people who will support and critique me and give me the opportunity to push myself in new and interesting directions.

Whether I hear back from the company who reached out at the end of last year or not, their offer has lit a fire that I won't let burn out. This year I will achieve something significant, follow my passions and make myself proud.
